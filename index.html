Hull Resident's Good Deed Turns Sour as Cat Rescued from Tree

Image: Man saving cat from a tree.

Author: Will Sparkes

13/08/2025, 13:09

A daring rescue mission was undertaken by local firefighters in Hull yesterday evening after a cat became stranded in a tree on Elmwood Road. The feline, a grey and white tom cat, had apparently climbed up the tree in the early hours of the morning, much to the dismay of its owners. Witnesses reported that the cat had been terrorizing the neighbourhood for weeks, knocking over trash cans and scratching garden furniture. Despite this, a group of well-meaning residents called for the cat's rescue, citing concerns for the animal's safety.

However, not everyone in the neighbourhood was pleased with the outcome of the rescue mission. Several neighbours, who wished to remain anonymous, revealed that they had actually placed the cat in the tree as a means of ridding themselves of the pest. "We were so fed up with the cat's antics that we thought it was the best solution," said one neighbour. "But now, it seems like everyone is celebrating the cat's rescue, without considering the fact that we were just trying to do what was best for our community." The cat has since been returned to its relieved owners, but the controversy surrounding its rescue has left many in the neighbourhood scratching their heads.
